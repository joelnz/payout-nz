Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about final pay and leave entitlements in NZ.

Wait, what exactly is "Final Pay"?

It's the very last payment your employer gives you when you leave your job. It's not just your last week's wages—it also includes any holiday days you haven't used, any days in lieu you've earned, and sometimes a portion of your yearly bonus if you're lucky. Basically, it’s all the money they still owe you up to your last official minute of work.

When do I actually get the money?

Usually, you get it on your next regular payday after your last day of work. Your employer can’t wait months to pay you, but they also don't have to pay you the very second you walk out the door. It just has to be on or before the normal payday for your final period of work.

Will I get paid for unused sick leave?

Unfortunately, no. In New Zealand, the law says you don't get paid out for sick leave or bereavement leave when you leave. You only get paid out for "Annual Leave" (holidays) and "Alternative Holidays" (days in lieu).

I've worked here for less than a year. Do I get anything?

Yes! Even if you’ve only been there for a month, you are owed a 8% holiday pay bonus. This is because you haven't "unlocked" your 4 weeks of leave yet. Instead, the law says you get 8% of everything you've earned since your first day as a cash payout.

What if I'm "resigning immediately" (no notice)?

Be careful. If you don't give the notice length required in your contract, your employer might be able to deduct pay from your final payout (but only if your contract explicitly says they can). You should always check your agreement or talk to your boss first.

Does my final pay get taxed differently?

It feels like it! Because your final pay is often a "lump sum" (a big chunk at once), it can sometimes be taxed at a higher rate known as "Extra Pay." You aren't losing the money forever, though—if you overpay tax, you'll usually get a refund from the IRD at the end of the tax year.

What do I do if my boss refuses to pay me?

Speak up! First, send them a polite email asking for a breakdown. If they still won't pay, call Employment New Zealand (0800 20 90 20). They are a free government service that helps people get the pay they are legally owed.

For official government guidance, visit employment.govt.nz.
